Welcome!

My name is Neha Nepal and I graduated with a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from UCSB in June, 2026. Born in Nepal and raised in the United States since the age of seven, I have always been driven by curiosity about how things move, interact, and function.

Although I entered college with a strong focus on aerospace engineering, my experience in UCSB’s Mechanical Engineering program broadened my perspective. Collaborating with peers whose interests span bioengineering, mechatronics, and energy systems exposed me to new applications of engineering principles. Through this environment, my passion evolved beyond aerospace alone to a deeper interest in heat transfer and fluid mechanics as well as an interest in robotics and controls.

I have gained both research and hands-on experience in fluid dynamics and heat transfer, developing a strong analytical foundation alongside practical problem-solving skills. I am particularly interested in applying these experiences, along with my passion for robotics, to industry roles. I am excited to contribute to innovative technologies where fluidics, heat transfer, and intelligent systems intersect.
