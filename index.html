Welcome!

My name is Neha Nepal, and I am a fourth-year Mechanical Engineering student at the University of California, Santa Barbara. Born in Nepal and raised in the United States since the age of seven, I have always been driven by curiosity about how things move, interact, and function.

Since my freshman year of high school, attending UCSB had been a dream of mine. Initially, I planned to pursue aerospace engineering and applied to universities accordingly. By my senior year, I realized UCSB did not offer an aerospace engineering program, and it dropped to my third choice. However, after visiting campus, I recognized that UCSB was the right fit for me academically and personally. I ultimately chose UCSB and enrolled in Mechanical Engineering, a decision that has shaped and strengthened my aspirations in unexpected ways.

Although I entered college with a strong focus on aerospace engineering, my experience in UCSB’s Mechanical Engineering program broadened my perspective. Collaborating with peers whose interests span bioengineering, mechatronics, and energy systems exposed me to new applications of engineering principles. Through this environment, my passion evolved beyond aerospace alone to a deeper interest in heat transfer and fluid mechanics more broadly.

I have gained both research and hands-on experience in fluid dynamics and heat transfer, developing a strong analytical foundation alongside practical problem-solving skills. I am particularly interested in applying these experiences, along with my passion for robotics, to industry roles involving fluid systems, thermal systems, or fluid-structure interactions. I am excited to contribute to innovative technologies where fluidics, heat transfer, and intelligent systems intersect.
