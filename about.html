About Me

How I Think as an Engineer

I’m a Mechanical Engineer who enjoys turning complex technical problems into practical, testable designs through analysis, iteration, and hands-on validation.

My Engineering Approach

I approach engineering by first understanding the system: what it needs to do, what constraints matter, and how success will be measured. From there, I use CAD, calculations, simulation, and prototyping to move from concept to validation.

My strongest interests are in mechanical systems where thermal, fluid, structural, and motion-based constraints interact. I enjoy work that requires both analytical thinking and hands-on execution.

What I’m Drawn To

Aerospace Systems

I’m interested in spacecraft, aircraft, and defense systems where reliability, environment, materials, and mechanical performance are critical.

Robotics & Mechatronics

I enjoy designing systems that move, sense, actuate, and respond to real physical conditions.

Thermal & Fluid Systems

Heat transfer and fluid mechanics are areas I continue to return to because they connect theory directly to real system performance.

Engineering Timeline

Mechanical Engineering at UC Santa Barbara Built a foundation in design, thermodynamics, fluids, heat transfer, controls, mechatronics, and simulation.
Hands-On Design Projects Designed and prototyped mechanical systems including an autonomous window-cleaning robot, machined air motor, embedded rep counter, and MATLAB-based engineering simulations.
Undergraduate Research Developed MATLAB analysis tools and worked with telemetry/GPS data for fluid dynamics research focused on marine drag reduction.
Industry Experience Supported commercial HVAC engineering projects at Western Allied Mechanical, gaining experience with mechanical drawings, field coordination, RFIs, submittals, and real-world system installation.
Northrop Grumman-Sponsored Capstone Led a five-person team designing a camera housing for spacecraft thermal chamber testing under extreme temperature and vacuum conditions.
